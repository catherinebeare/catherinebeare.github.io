Binomials can be a tricky topic. Binomials are a fundamental topic in many pure mathematics topics. This page aims to help you with your understanding of binomials.

What is a Binomial?

A binomial is an algebraic expression that contains two different terms connected by addition or subtraction.

Which of the following is a binomial expression?

\( 3x^2 \) consists of one term, and is not connected by addition or subtraction (This is called a monomial). \( 2x^3 + 3y^5 - 1 \) consists of 3 terms (This is an example of a trinomial). \( 4x + 7 \) is an algebraic expression containing two different terms which are connected by addition.

Factorial!

What is factorial? Factorial (denoted "!") is the product of an integer and all the integers below it. For example,

\[ 5! = 5 \times 4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1 = 120 \]

What is the value of 4!?

\(4! = 4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1 = 24.\)

Now, let \(n\) = the total number of events

and \(k\) = the number of desired outcomes.

Then we can say the number of ways to get a specific outcome is “n choose k”. We write \[\binom{n}{k}=\frac{n!}{k!\left(n-k\right)!}.\] These values can be calculated using the "\(nCr\)" button on your calulator.

These are the coefficients in a binomial expansion.

What are the values of \(n\) and \(k\) if we want to flip a coin six times and get heads four of those times?

We need the number of times we want to get heads (our specific outcome), so we have \(k=4\). The number of events is the total number of coin tosses, giving us \(n=6\). Therefore, there are \(\binom{6}{4} = 15 \) ways to get four heads from six coin tosses.

Binomial Theorem

The Binomial theorem is given by

\[(x+a)^n = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k}x^k a^{n-k},\]

where \(n\) = the number of trials

\(k\) = the number of successes

\(x\) = the probability of success on a single trial

\(a\) = the probability of failure on a single trial
